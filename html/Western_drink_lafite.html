Chateau lafite-rothschild

Chateau lafite-rothschild is a red wine produced by Chateau Lafite, one of the five famous chateaus in bordeaux, France.

Lafite wine can have the world's top quality, of course, the first is lafite manor soil and the local Micro Climate advantaged. Chateau lafite has a total area of 90 hectares and each hectare is planted with 8,500 vines. Among them, Cabernet Sauvignon (Cabernet Sauvignon) accounts for about 70%, Merlot for about 20%, and the rest are Cabernet Franc. The average tree age is over forty years. The annual production is about 30,000 cases of wine (12 bottles of 750ml per case). This output ranks the crown of all the world's top famous zhuang. Compared with this output and the prices it can sustain, lafite's achievement is unmatched. Chateau lafite's grapes are grown in a very traditional way, with little use of chemicals or fertilizers, and are carefully tended until they are fully ripe. Skilled workers pick grapes from trees and screen them. Grapes are picked and sent to the press for secondary screening by more advanced technical workers to ensure that each grape pressed meets the requirements of high quality. It takes two to three vines in lafite to produce a bottle of 750ml. In order to protect these valuable vines, if there is no special agreement of the head office, chateau lafite is generally not allowed to be visited by others. In addition, chateau lafite is famous for hiring top winemakers. The character of lafite wine is gentle wan fine, relatively introverted, unlike the strong personality of two daimyo zhuang latour and king wudang produced in pu yi le village. Lafite has outstanding floral and fruity aroma, so many wine lovers call lafite the "queen" of the wine kingdom.
